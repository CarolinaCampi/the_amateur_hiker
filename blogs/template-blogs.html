6 London day trips

Even though I’ve been wanting to visit Cornwall ever since watching About time, I was recently reminded of this fact while binge-watching the delightful first season of The Bucaneers. The dramatic coastline cut by staggering cliffs could possibly captivate just about anyone, including me. So, it was easy to choose it as the destination for a 4-day getaway from London. Here, I will share my itinerary along with practical information about the attractions included as well as a few of my opinions.

4-day itinerary

Recommendations and logistics for the Tintagel castle visit
  • Buy the ticket before your trip in the website of the castle to save 15% off your tickets: https://www.english-heritage.org.uk/visit/places/tintagel-castle/
  • Opening times (depending on the season): 10 to 16-18hs
    • IMPORTANT: Opening days and times depend on the season so check on the website when planning your Cornwall getaway.
    • For example: in winter, off peak season, the castle closes on weekdays.
    • https://www.english-heritage.org.uk/visit/places/tintagel-castle/prices-and-opening-times/
  • Price: starting at 14.50 pounds for adults and 8.60 pounds for children
  • Duration of visit: 2-3hs
  • Notes: there is quite a bit of walking involved, most of which is on uneven terrain. So, take appropriate footwear and rain-proof layers, if the weather requires it.
  • The access to the castle includes a road with a steep incline. You can hire a van to take you down (and up when leaving) the access road.
  • If the weather is nice, there is an open beach by Merlin's Cave where you can take a dip.

Recommendation: pack some lunch and snacks and take advantage of the picnic tables near Fingle Bridge.
